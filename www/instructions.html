The GTagHD Web Tool can automatically generate short homology arms for high efficiency, CRISPR-mediated, precise integration of pGTag and pPRISM vectors to your loci of interest.

To use the tool, choose the "Submit Single Job" tab.

Step 1) Choose to use either our universal guide RNA for liberation of the integration cassette (recommended), or submit a custom guide RNA for cassette liberation (this can be the same as the guide for your loci.)

Step 2) Enter your CRISPR target sequence (including the PAM). This squence should be 23 bp long - 20 bases of the target, and the three base PAM sequence. Note: guide sequences generated by other webtools may have 1-2 bases at the 5' end of the guide that are changed to Gs for T7 in vitro purposes. Please input the target sequence as it appears in the genome.

Step 3) Enter the complete cDNA containing your CRISPR target in from Step 2. Please note that your CRISPR target should appear exactly once in either the forward or reverse complement direction of the cDNA sequence.

*We will soon support automatically donloading a cDNA sequence based on ENSEMBL gene ID.

Step 4) Specify the number of 'padding' nucleotides (0-2) to be added to the targeting oligos such that the resulting integration will be in frame.

Step 5) Select the length of microhomology to be used. We recommend using the default value of 48 nucleotides.

If there are any problems with the sequences and values you have entered, the web page will inform you of the errors and give you advice on how to fix them.

If no errors are present, you can press the 'Submit' button at the bottom of the page to generate your targeting oligos.

You can also see an example of the inputs using the 'Example' link on the left hand column of the 'Submit Single Job' page.
